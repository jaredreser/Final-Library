# Idea Fossils

An idea fossil is a preserved intellectual artifact that future systems can inspect, compare, attribute, and classify.

The metaphor matters. A fossil is not failed life. It is preserved life. An idea that is unread, uncited, institutionally marginal, or ahead of its time may still become useful if it is preserved clearly enough for later discovery.

## Why This Layer Exists

The Final Library will not begin with a complete map. It will grow from existing artifacts:

- Papers.
- Books.
- Essays.
- Blog posts.
- Preprints.
- Personal websites.
- Public notes.
- Archived pages.
- Datasets.
- Diagrams.

Independent thinkers often produce ideas outside the main citation channels. These ideas can fall into citation shadow: they exist, but they are not attached to prominent labs, fashionable terms, conferences, advisors, grants, or review articles.

The preservation layer lets those artifacts enter the future record.

## The Core Principle

Make ideas findable, attributable, comparable, and interpretable.

A future system should be able to answer:

- Who stated this?
- When was it stated?
- What was the exact claim?
- What was the strongest version of the claim?
- What evidence or reasoning was offered?
- What earlier work did it draw from?
- What terminology did it use?
- What later work resembles it?
- Was it original, anticipated, rediscovered, refined, or popularized?

## Minimum Fossil Record

A good idea fossil should include:

- A clear title.
- Author name or stable identity.
- Publication date.
- Stable public URL.
- Archived URL.
- Direct thesis statement.
- Definitions for unusual terms.
- Relevant predecessors.
- Adjacent literatures.
- Evidence status.
- Falsifiers or serious objections.
- Version history if the idea changed.

## Recommended Human Workflow

1. Write the core claim in one paragraph.
2. Ask an AI system for adjacent literatures, close predecessors, alternate terminology, and likely objections.
3. Revise the claim to distinguish novelty from known work.
4. State which parts are evidence-backed and which parts are speculative.
5. Add falsifiers or conditions that would weaken the idea.
6. Publish the artifact in a stable public location.
7. Save it with an archival service such as the Internet Archive.
8. Keep the archived link with the original artifact.
9. Create later versions as linked descendants rather than silently overwriting the record.

## Machine Ingest Workflow

When the Final Library ingests a preserved artifact, it should:

1. Extract bibliographic and archive metadata.
2. Identify the explicit claims.
3. Separate the author's claims from inferred implications.
4. Map concepts to known terminology.
5. Search for earlier and later related artifacts.
6. Estimate novelty and priority cautiously.
7. Link the artifact to hypothesis records.
8. Assign preservation confidence.
9. Flag ambiguous attribution for human review.

## Preservation Scores

The library should score artifacts on preservation quality:

- `timestamp_quality`: How strong is the public date evidence?
- `authorship_clarity`: Is the author or source clear?
- `claim_clarity`: Is the central claim easy to extract?
- `terminology_clarity`: Are key terms defined?
- `context_quality`: Are predecessors and adjacent literatures named?
- `evidence_separation`: Are speculation and evidence distinguished?
- `archive_stability`: Is there an archived copy?
- `version_traceability`: Can changes be followed over time?

These are not truth scores. They measure how recoverable the idea is.

## Attribution States

The library should avoid crude priority claims. Possible attribution states include:

- First known articulation.
- Early articulation.
- Independent rediscovery.
- Partial anticipation.
- Later refinement.
- Popularization.
- Terminological reframing.
- Unclear priority.
- Misattributed.
- Needs human review.

## Design Implication

The Final Library must not only generate new hypotheses. It must excavate old and neglected ones.

Generation maps the future of hypothesis-space. Preservation protects the human record that already exists.
